ABOUT:


RESULTS

The Faster Mustache 24 Hour Urban Bicycle Relay is a relay like no other. Its a full 24 hours of bicycles, friends, and fun. Teams of 4-6 will take base at 97 Estoria while riders relay the streets of Atlanta, fighting traffic, weather, hills and exhaustion. Back at camp, teams will rest, relax, watch movies, play games, do side competitions, and party with the 97 Estoria crowd. You will get the chance to ride Atlanta more than you want, and meet lots of great people. You're invited!

Great prizes will be offered and great fun will be had. Simply sign up and show up on time for registration.

Whats it going to cost me?

Participation in the relay will cost $10 per person:
4 person team - $40
5 person team - $50
6 person team - $60

This money is used to fund relay promotion, signage, t-shirts, prizes, etc. Any extra money will be donated to charity. This is not a fund raiser or a profit endeavor. We are here to have fun and to promote riding within our city.

What does this money get me?

Other than being allowed to participate in this SWEET relay, your entry fee also gets you a t-shirt and free brunch on Sunday morning, provided by 97 Estoria. And i wouldn't be so sure that there wont be some additional free food and fun to be had that will be paid for with your fee.

Who are the teams?

Teams are made up of 4-6 people. Teams can be a group of friends, coworkers, enemies, etc. All you need is the people, bikes, and a name for yourself. Sign up online to let us know you are coming, then registration is the day of the relay (Saturday morning at 9:00am.) All members do not need to be present at the start of the relay (we realize some people may be working during the span of 24 hours), but all riders must have signed the liability form to ride. And each rider must complete at least one lap for your team to be considered legitimate.

Browse the rest of the site and then sign up! Also, feel free to email us with questions/comments!

What is Faster Mustache?

Faster Mustache is an independent organization made up of a diverse group of Atlanta residents with one thing in common: riding bicycles. Fixed or free, geared or single, cruiser, chopper, racer, clunker, we ride 'em. Our interests are as diverse as our riders but we are all dedicated to promoting safer riding conditions in Atlanta and being ambassadors for Atlanta's road bicycle community.
